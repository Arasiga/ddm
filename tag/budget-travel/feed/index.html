]]> Hong Kong was my first official solo trip and I loved every moment of it. As a first-time solo female traveler, I was looking for a safe Southeast Asian country to visit. Hong Kong was a perfect destination for my first solo trip! The crime rates here are one of the lowest in the world and the city is prefect blend of East-meets-West.

Here are my tips for the solo traveler looking to visit Hong Kong on their own!

When deciding where to stay I usually do two things: First, I go to Hostelworld and look up the city I’m travelling to. Second, I filter the reviews to 8.0 or higher then sort by price. From there I go down the list reading reviews and comparing the location. I try and get one close to city center where most of the attractions are. For Hong Kong, I stayed a bit outside of the city center but with the great metro system I was only a short ride away. Yesinn at Causeaway Bay was a great find with wonderful staff, amenities and friendly travelers!

Getting Around

As mentioned above, the transit system here is great! I picked up a tourist Octopus card that allowed me to load money and travel on buses, trams, and the subway. I booked mine ahead of time and got it at the airport using Klook. It came preloaded with $50 HKD with no deposit fee – meaning you can take the card home! And don’t worry, if you have left over money it can be refunded. (Although I didn’t because it seemed like too much of a hassle for $5.)

 

Where to Eat

If you are trying to save on cash, eat at local stalls or shops because they are very affordable, delicious and friendly. If you decide to check out the fishing village, Tai O, be sure to try fish sui mai and fresh cuddle fish. When walking around the markets, find a stand that looks busy and pick anything that looks good. Go for something new and get outside of your comfort zone!

Explore Hong Kong

One of my favorite things to do in a new city is just walk around the neighborhoods with no goal in mind. If you are near the city center, you will often find many markets or local food stalls. If you don’t like the idea of that, sign up for a free walking tour! The tour guide will take you around the main areas and teach you a lot about the history and culture.

 

Go on an Adventure

For me, I love the craziness of a big city mixed with the serenity of nature. Contrary to the popular belief that Hong Kong is a concrete jungle, there are actually plenty of greenery and great hikes all over to get away from the rush of the city. I preferred Dragons Back over Victoria Peak as it was less touristy and crowded so I really got to escape the city and enjoy my walk.

 

Nightlife

I love going out in new places and seeing how the party scene changes from one place to another. In Hong Kong, there are two main nightlife areas: Lan Kwai Fong in Central, and Lockhart Road in Wan Chai – both will give you a wild night! Bar hopping is the best way to see the area. If you get there before 9/10:00 pm you can make it to happy hour and save a few bucks. I have to say, my favourite place to drink wasn’t a local bar but actually Mr. Wong’s, an all you can eat and DRINK (beer) restaurant. This is a popular spot for travelers and exchange students so when I showed up alone, a table of fellow travelers welcomed me with open arms. We ate and played drinking games into the night!

Hong Kong Itinerary

Hong Kong is definitely a city I’m looking forward to returning to one day. There is so much to see and do, I barely scratched the surface on my own. I highly recommend any solo travelers packing their bags and making the trip. For a more detailed itinerary and how I managed a budget getaway to Hong Kong, check out my 3 day budget itinerary!

I spent my last few hours learning more about the culture and history of the island all for FREE admission. After 3 days of early mornings and late nights I was exhausted and ready to get back! This weekend trip was the perfect amount of time to squeeze in a holiday on a working schedule and a wonderful place to get introduced into solo travelling.

Avoid the summer months if you want to avoid long queues and large crowds. European tourist attractions can be a mess during peak season. 2. Do as the locals do

Try and blend in with the locals, don’t  stand out as an obnoxious tourist with bad manners. Be aware of your surroundings and try to follow what the locals do: grab a beer (doesn’t matter what time) or watch the local sport match.

 

 

 

3. Travel by train

Travelling by train may be more efficient and easier than air travel when exploring Europe. There are lots of options when it comes to booking your tickets: you can do it in person, at a kiosk, online, or use a rail planner app. You can also find deals and sales when it comes to using a Eurail pass.

 

 

 

 

4. Use budget airlines

In Europe there’s no shortage of budget airlines with companies such as Ryanair or easyJet. Travelling around Europe by plane can be faster and sometimes even be cheaper. Airfares can often be cheaper than a train ticket if you book in advance, travel in the middle of the week, and pay attention to additional fees. Packing light or taking just a carry on will help you avoid additional fees!

 

 

 

 

5. Wear comfortable shoes

One of the best ways to explore any city is to walk everywhere! Long days venturing from attraction to attraction will have feet and back aching if you aren’t wearing comfortable walking shoes, these are a must!

 

 

 

6. Carry cash 

While you don’t want to carry all your cash on you, you don’t want to rely only on credit cards as it is not uncommon for a place in Europe to only accept cash. Many small local business, restaurants, markets or taxis will only take cash.

 

 

7. Stay longer in one place

Staying in one place longer will allow you to experience the city more like a local and get to know the neighborhood. You’ll have a chance to meet more people and find your favourite spots in the city. Staying put also means less packing and unpacking and more time to explore and relax!

 

 

 

8. Wonder off the beaten path

I mean you’re not, not going to see the Eiffel Tower when in Paris but sometimes in your free time seeing the lesser known side of a city can be just as rewarding. Talk to locals they’ll have the inside scoop on all the best places to check out!

 

 

 

 

9. Stay central 

Although accommodation may be a bit more pricey its worth the extra cost to easily get to all the best attractions the city center has to offer. You’ll be able to find bars and restaurants much easier and avoid the hassle of transportation if you stay central.  If you want to stay a little bit outside of the central area, make sure public transportation is available near by.

 

 

 

 

10. Free city walking tours

Almost every major city has started to offer “Free Walking Tours” this concept allows you to take the tour then tip the tour guide an amount that you see fit. Are you still patiently waiting for your letter from Hogwarts? Prepared to drop out of college to attend a school for witchcraft and wizardry? Me too!

 

 

Being only an hour bus ride away from the Warner Bros. Studio Tour we just had to visit “The Making of Harry Potter”. This tour features costumes,  props, how special effects are used, facts about the actors and film crew, and complete movie sets from the filming of all 8 movies.

Stepping into the studio tour was like stepping into the movies.  The tour starts off with a short film about the experience and the franchise and then the doors to the Great Hall are opened.  This massive set is decorated depending on the current season. For us, we went in October and were treated to a Halloween feast with the gold plates full and Jack-o-lanterns hanging from the enchanted ceiling.

When you walk through the tour, there is everything from the “Sorting Hat” to the “Forbidden Forest”. The studio tour is all set up  for us muggles to explore! Here are some helpful tips to help you plan your magical getaway.

 

 

Booking tickets in advance 

Booking your tickets online in advance is required because they do not sell tickets at the door due to a limited number of visitors that can visit the Studio Tour at any one time. They do this to  ensure you have the best experience possible! Now if you are on exchange like us, chances are your school/ other students will run a trip here at least once a term so keep your eyes open! We went to the tour with a student-led group that had our travel and tickets covered for only £20.00, saving us nearly $50 CAN.

Standard Ticket Prices:

  • Adult (Ages 16 years and above) £41.00
  • Child (Ages 5 to 15 years) £33.00

Group rates and family passes are also available.

 

 

Getting there

The Studio is located almost 45 minutes outside of central London, but don’t worry, getting there isn’t hard. If you plan on driving, there is free parking available by showing your booking confirmation. Driving as a foreigner is not always easy so I suggest taking the train to Watford Junction station where shuttle buses run round trip for only £2.50 (cash only).

 

When you arrive

It is recommended you arrive 20 minutes prior to your ticketed time. Before your tour begins, you will have access to the Lobby Café, Studio Shop, cloakroom, and toilet facilities. Keep in mind, there is no food during the tour but a break about half way through in the back-lot allows you to picnic if you brought food. You can also buy food there or try some Harry Potter themed treats like Butterbeer!

 

Souvenirs 

As you can imagine the gift shop has everything imaginable related to Harry Potter and the Wizarding world. You may break the bank if you go in blind, so have a budget and some specific ideas of things you want to bring home. And if you are on a student budget (no budget), the good news is you can take  photos throughout the tour to save all your memories!

Are you a Harry Potter fan? If you aren’t, we kindly ask you to please re-evaluate your life.

]]> Ladies, this one is for you:  now we are all about equality and feminism but sometimes you just have to work with what you were given, and that’s not a bad thing! We are going to give you some of our best tips for going out on the town without spending any money.

Do your research

Exploring a city for the first time and not sure where to go?  There are tones of online resources available to help you find exactly the kind of night you are looking for. For example, DesignMyNight is a good one we used every time we went out in the UK. We found this great place in London, Ballie Ballerson, that has a fun ball pit to play at the bar!

Use social media

Once you got a few names from this search head over to the bar/club’s Facebook or other social media to see if they have any events running and past reviews on their site. Keep in mind, when reading reviews a lot will be negative as more people tend to write a review after having a bad experience rather than after having a great night.

Guest list

From the bars website or over Facebook, look for a bar with guest list which usually gets you in for free and sometimes even skip the line. All it takes is submitting your name through a quick message to their Facebook or submitting a form online. When you do this, typically you have to go early and the bar may be dead when you arrive but trust me, it will pick up! It’s totally worth it if you don’t want to pay for cover.

Keep your eyes open

Most people will tell you to walk with your head up, but I’m going to tell you the opposite. Because if you keep your head down and your eyes open then there’s a better chance you will be the lucky one to find that $10 bill (or whatever currency the country you’re in uses) on the ground.  Trust me, there’s no better feeling then finding that bill and running straight to the bar for drinks!

Free drinks

Drinks are always good, but free drinks are even better. Just be aware when getting drinks from strangers at the bar. Never accept a drink you didn’t see the bar tender make as unfortunate as it is, it is so easy for someone to slip something in it

Find the bottles

Bottle service is your friend. So many good nights we’ve had by meeting groups with bottle service there to celebrate. Especially as a traveler, meeting new people is what it’s all about …. And maybe you’ll get a free drink after the introduction.

Bring the bottles

That sparkly flask your friends got you for your 19th birthday, well now is the time to use it. Now almost all bars don’t allow outside drinks so you know… don’t get caught. And when you need chase to wash down your shots ask for a lemon slice over paying for chase, they’re free.
